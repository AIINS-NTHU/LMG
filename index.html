Layered Mesh-Gaussian (LMG): Efficient Streaming of Layered Mesh–Gaussian 3D Scenes

Abstract

High-quality interactive 3D experiences for XR require streaming large scenes under constrained and fluctuating network conditions. Existing mesh or 3D Gaussian splatting (3DGS) approaches each exhibit trade-offs among visual fidelity, model size, and start-up latency, while prior hybrid mesh–Gaussian methods largely focus on reconstruction quality rather than end-to-end delivery. In this work, we introduce LMG, a Layered Mesh–Gaussian streaming system designed for efficient delivery of large static 3D scenes. LMG represents each scene using a textured mesh as a streamable base layer and attaches surface-aligned Gaussians as an enhancement layer, with a training pipeline that leverages mesh geometry and texture cues to optimize compact, detail-preserving Gaussians. Built on this representation, LMG employs a DASH-based streaming pipeline that first delivers the mesh to provide an immediate, coherent scene overview, and then incrementally refines only the visible regions with Gaussian tiles according to the user’s viewport and available bandwidth. A joint bandwidth scheduler allocates bitrate across mesh and Gaussian layers to minimize start-up latency while maximizing perceptual quality. Experiments show that our layered representation achieves higher visual quality than mesh-only baselines at comparable storage or bitrate and produces more compact models than 3DGS-only scenes. End-to-end evaluations further demonstrate that LMG reduces start-up delay and rebuffering while improving viewport quality over monolithic 3DGS streaming and mesh-only streaming, making it a practical solution for scalable 3D scene delivery in XR.
